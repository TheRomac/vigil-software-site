Vintage Reader

A pocket e-reader that pretends, with complete sincerity, to be a battered old e-ink device.

One quiet reading app with a skeuomorphic e-ink soul: page dissolves, a three-shelf library with an A–Z rail, highlights and notes, bookmarks, a two-page spread, OpenDyslexic, scheduled light/dark, a browse-mode page gallery, comic zoom, and a built-in book service that fetches public-domain books from Project Gutenberg. Everything stays on your device; the wire is used only at your asking.

Reads

EPUB · TXT · Markdown · HTML · FB2 · CBZ comics · DRM-free Kindle (MOBI/AZW/PRC). DRM'd books are declined honestly.

Free, or ad-free

Free — the whole reader, every format, your whole library. Supported by a few unobtrusive ads: a small promo on the library shelf and an occasional full-screen ad at chapter breaks. Never while you're mid-page.

Ad-free (Premium) — a one-time Remove ads purchase turns every ad off for good: uninterrupted, quiet reading. No subscription, no accounts, nothing leaves your device.
